Mina the hollower is a Zelda like hollow knight like souls like action adventure, or basically its  a hard game based on Zelda with aspects of a Metroid Vania sprinkled with a combat system unique to itself

when you start the game the whole ordeal seems to be very linear +, until you reach the the main city halls from where u can practically go anywhere you like. This is very reminiscent of the original Dark Souls where after reaching Firelink Shrine u can pretty much go in any direction you desire. However unlike firelink shrine, the first area in mina is exceptionaly large. one thing that always blows me are the winding paths. found a new region? oh im sure it connects to everywhere one tunnel and you will be back  to the capital.

some thing that was very evidently copied fom hollow knight was its charms system which silksong jumps over. in mina they are called "Trinkets" and allow the players to add temporary upgrades to the player. 
Now thats where one of the issues (probably the only) of this games shows up. the problem is there are practically no movement upgrades in this game  and only temporary trinket upgrades which can be added or removed as the player so desires . its not reeaallly an issue but these do take up spaces  especially as certain trinkets would be permanant upgrades in some games

buuut u can buy a lot of trinket slots so its balances , another "issue" people say is  the difficulty which lets be real is not all that bad. after fixing the first of your choice spark tower , and getting some bones you can level up quite a bit and upgrade your health from the tall seller dude and also upgrade or try new weapons. oh and theres the whole "adjust difficulty" thing right there for the struggling (which honestly shouldnt be there)

as of right now I'm on my third spark tower and the game is really good and reminds me of my HK and Silksong playthroughs
